About this quiz

This quiz checks your understanding of two foundational feminist texts: bell hooks' Feminism Is for Everybody: Passionate Politics (2000) and Betty Friedan's The Feminine Mystique (1963). It covers hooks' definition of feminism and her arguments about class, race, the body, and masculinity, as well as Friedan's account of "the problem that has no name" and the post-war myth of the happy housewife.

  • Each attempt draws 10 questions at random from a 30-question bank: 5 true/false and 5 multiple-choice, spanning both books.
  • Answer every question, then press Submit answers.
  • You'll see your score plus an explanation for each question, drawn straight from the books.
  • Press Retake quiz for a fresh set of questions.
